Privacy

Last updated: May 11, 2026

Short version: Vesper is a single HTML file running in your browser. I don't have servers, I don't have analytics, I don't see what you type. Your data lives in your browser's local storage and — if you turn on sync — in a JSON file you put in your own cloud folder. The one exception, all opt-in: if you turn on Smart clustering, your browser fetches a small ML model once from a public CDN and then runs it locally on your device. That's the whole thing.

What Vesper stores about you

Everything you do in Vesper — your task names, focus blocks, brain-dump entries, energy check-ins, intentions, reflections, contacts, settings — is saved to localStorage in your own browser. Nothing leaves your device unless you explicitly turn on a feature that sends it somewhere.

Features that touch the network

These are all opt-in. Vesper works completely offline without any of them.

  • Weather backgrounds — if you grant location permission, your latitude and longitude (rounded to 4 decimals, ~10m precision) are sent to Open-Meteo to fetch current weather. The same coordinates are also sent (rounded to 2 decimals, ~1km) to BigDataCloud's free client-side reverse-geocoding endpoint to turn the coordinates into a human-readable place name ("Seattle, WA") for display. Both services are free, no accounts. They publish their own privacy practices.
  • File-based sync — if you pick a sync file, the file lives in whatever cloud folder you put it in (iCloud Drive, Dropbox, Google Drive, OneDrive). Vesper writes your data there. The privacy of that file is governed by your cloud provider's terms. The file is plain JSON; I recommend keeping it in a private folder.
  • Smart clustering (experimental, off by default) — if you turn this on in Settings, Vesper downloads a tiny sentence-embedding model (Xenova/all-MiniLM-L6-v2, ~25 MB) from jsDelivr, a public CDN that mirrors npm packages and HuggingFace model files. The download happens once and is cached in your browser. After that, the model runs entirely on your device — your brain-dump text is never sent over the network. The library is Transformers.js by HuggingFace; the model is by the Xenova community port of all-MiniLM-L6-v2. jsDelivr publishes its own privacy practices. To stop using it, untick the setting; your existing data stays in place.
  • Browser notifications — if you enable browser notifications, alerts are scheduled and fired by your browser. No external service is involved.
  • Service worker — Vesper installs a service worker for offline use. It caches the app's HTML and icons. It does not send anything anywhere.

What I do not collect

  • No analytics, no telemetry, no usage tracking
  • No accounts, no email addresses, no passwords
  • No cookies, no fingerprinting, no advertising IDs
  • No connection to a backend I control — I don't run any server

If you support Vesper via GitHub Sponsors

If you choose to tip via the "Support Vesper" link in Settings, you'll be sent to GitHub Sponsors. That transaction is between you and GitHub. I only see what GitHub shows me: your username, your tier, and any public note you choose to send. Their privacy policy applies.

Data deletion

To delete everything, open Settings → Your data and use Export JSON (if you want a backup first), then either clear your browser's storage for the site, or delete your localStorage entry called onething.v2. If you have a sync file, delete the file from your cloud folder. That's it — there's nothing for me to delete on a server because there is no server.

Contact

If you have a privacy question, open an issue at github.com/scotsf90-ux/focus.

Changes

If Vesper ever does collect something — for instance if a future paid feature requires it — I'll update this page and add a clear notice in the app before turning it on.
