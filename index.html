A tree that grows from your habits

Each focus block adds a branch. Each consistent week fills it with leaves. Each evening reflection drops fruit. Long streaks get a warm halo. Your accumulated effort, made visible.

☀

Breaks that push you outside

Every break suggestion biases toward leaving the chair: "Walk to the furthest window." "Text one person who isn't a coworker." "Watch the sunset — even 30 seconds is the right amount." Time-of-day aware.

🪶

Brain dump that doesn't break your focus

Stray thought during a block? Type it in the parking lot, hit Enter, keep working. It waits. Each dump drops a small leaf at your tree's base — even the off-task thoughts count.

📝

Bookended days: intention + reflection

Morning: "what do you want today to feel like?" Evening: "one sentence — what went well?" Both surface together in your week view. Quiet ceremony, not a journal habit you'll abandon.

🌧

A living world behind your work

The sky shifts through dawn to dusk. Real weather where you are (with permission) brings rain, snow, stars at night. A forest hill with breathing grass. Subtle — never competes with the timer.

📁

Your data lives on your devices

Pick a sync file in iCloud Drive / Dropbox — opens on every device, syncs through your own cloud. No account, no server, no email. Or export to JSON / a calendar feed at any time.

Why Vesper exists

Most focus tools are timers with badges. They reward you for sitting still and looking at the screen — exactly what an ADHD brain doesn't need more of.

Vesper is built around the premise that the break is the point. You sit at a desk all day. You love being outside. You love people. The work matters but it's not the whole life. The timer is a way to give yourself permission to focus AND a kind, repeated nudge to leave the chair.

And the tree growing from your real habits is the part that's hard to leave — your accumulated effort, your reflections, the days you actually showed up, made into a small living thing in the corner.

If Vesper helps you, you can pitch in

Vesper is free, forever, for everyone. No accounts, no paywall, no upgrade nag — every feature on this page is yours, and the ones I build next will be too.

If it earns a spot in your day and you'd like to support its quiet maintenance, there's a small "Support Vesper" link tucked inside Settings. Pay once, or a few dollars a month — whatever feels right. Or don't, and the tool still works the same. That's the whole deal.
